Batido de Mamey

Welcome to the ultimate guide for making a batido de mamey, which is Spanish for 'mamey shake'! Mamey is a fruit that is grown throughout Latin America, the Carribean, and South Florida, and it looks like a football on its exterior. When you cut open the fruit, however, you'll see a red-colored meat that tastes like a combination of honey, brown sugar, caramel, and cinnamon. Follow this recipe to create delicious mamey shake that everyone will love.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of fresh mamey
  • 1 cup milk
  • 3 tablespoons condensed milk
  • 3 tablespoons of sugar
  • 1/2 cup crushed ice

Instructions

  1. Take out the mamey skin by cutting the mamey lengthwise and peel away.
  2. Scrape out the whitish part of the mamey until you reach the mamey's red flesh.
  3. Cut the flesh away from the mamey's large seed.
  4. Insert everything into the blender EXCEPT the ice, and blend until frothy . . . or to your desired consistency.
  5. Insert the crushed ice and blend until the ice is totally grounded and the shake is thick and rich.
